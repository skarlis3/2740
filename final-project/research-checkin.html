Final Project: Research Check-In

Due April 19 — Submit in Canvas

What This Is

A quick snapshot of where you are with finding sources. This isn't a formal assignment — it's a way for me to see that you're making progress and to catch any issues before you get too deep into building your site.

If you're stuck on finding sources, that's exactly what this check-in is for. Let me know what's not working and we'll figure it out.

What to Submit

Upload a document and/or use the Canvas text entry. Nothing formal needed — I just want to see what you're working with.

At Least Two Scholarly Sources

For each one, include:

  • Name of the source (article or book title)
  • Author
  • Type of source (journal article, book chapter, etc.)
  • How you plan to use it — a sentence or two on how it connects to your project. This doesn't need to be formal; just tell me what it gives you.

Not sure what counts as a scholarly source or where to look? See the Finding Sources guide.

Other Sources or Multimedia You're Considering

These don't need to be finalized — I just want to see what direction you're heading. Are you looking at documentaries, interviews, music, visual art, archival materials, websites? For each one you mention, include:

  • Name or description of the source
  • Type of source (video, podcast, image, website, etc.)
  • How you're thinking about using it — even "I'm not sure yet but it feels relevant" is fine at this stage.

Grading

Graded on the check system. If you submit at least two scholarly sources with the info above and share your thinking on other sources/multimedia, you'll get full credit. This is about showing progress, not perfection.
