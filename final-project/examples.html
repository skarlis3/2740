Final Project: Examples

The example below shows what a close reading and analysis can look like when it's built as a website instead of written as an essay. It's not from our course readings — it's based on a paper I wrote when I was a student — but it demonstrates the kind of thinking your project should include: pulling specific passages from a text, paying attention to language, and using a framework to drive the analysis.

Your project doesn't need to look exactly like this. Use it as a reference for the kind of work, not a template for the format.

  • Example Close Reading & Analysis

    A Marxist reading of Rebecca Harding Davis's Life in the Iron Mills, tracking how Davis uses the metaphor of hunger — from literal starvation to soul-starvation — to build toward the downfall of Hugh Wolfe. Built on Google Sites to show what's possible with that platform.

Previous student projects that cover the multimodal aspect of exploring a theme, framework, or historical era are linked on Canvas under the Final Project module, towards the top of the page.
