Using Google Sites

A guide to building your final project site

What This Covers

This guide walks you through the basics of Google Sites — creating a site, adding pages, adding content, and publishing. You don’t need any web design experience. If you can use Google Docs, you can use Google Sites.

If you’re using a different platform for your project, this page won’t apply directly, but the general concepts (pages, navigation, embedding media) are similar across most website builders.

Getting Started

Step 1: Go to sites.google.com and sign in with your Google account.

Step 2: Click the + button (or Blank site) to create a new site. You can also choose a template, but a blank site gives you the most flexibility.

Adding Pages

Your site will need multiple pages. Here’s how to add them:

Step 1: In the right sidebar, click the Pages tab.

Step 2: Click the + button at the bottom of the pages list to add a new page.

Step 3: Name the page and click Done. Your new page will automatically show up in your site’s navigation menu.

Navigation is automatic

Google Sites automatically creates a navigation menu based on your pages. You can reorder pages by dragging them in the Pages panel. The order in the panel is the order in the nav menu.

Adding Text

Option 1: Double-click anywhere on the canvas to start typing. A text box will appear.

Option 2: In the right sidebar under Insert, click Text box to place a text block on the page.

Once you’re typing, a formatting toolbar appears above the text box with options for headings, bold, italic, links, lists, and alignment — similar to Google Docs.

Tips for text

  • Use headings (Title, Heading, Subheading) to organize your content — don’t just make text bigger and bold.
  • Keep paragraphs relatively short. Websites are not essays — readers scan more than they read linearly.
  • Use the link tool (chain icon) to link between your own pages or to external sources.

Adding Images

In the right sidebar under Insert, click Images. You can:

  • Upload

    Upload an image from your computer.

  • By URL

    Paste the URL of an image from the web. Make sure you have the right to use it (Library of Congress, Wikimedia Commons, and other public domain sources are good options).

  • Google Drive

    Insert an image from your Google Drive.

Once the image is placed, click on it to resize, crop, or add alt text (a description for screen readers — please add this for accessibility).

Adding YouTube Videos

Step 1: In the right sidebar under Insert, click YouTube.

Step 2: Search for the video or paste a YouTube URL.

Step 3: Select the video and click Insert. The video will embed directly on your page — visitors can watch it without leaving your site.

Other embeds

You can also embed Google Docs, Slides, Maps, and other content using the Insert panel. The Embed option (under Insert → Embed) lets you paste embed codes from other sites too.

Sections & Layouts

Google Sites organizes content in sections — horizontal blocks that span the full width of the page. Each section can have its own layout (one column, two columns, etc.) and background color.

To add a new section, scroll to the bottom of your page and click the layout options that appear, or use Insert → Layouts from the sidebar.

Choose a layout:

Sections are useful for organizing content visually — for example, you might have a section with text on the left and an image on the right, or a full-width section for a video.

Changing the Look

Click the Themes tab in the right sidebar to change your site’s color scheme and fonts. Pick something that fits your project’s tone — but prioritize readability over style. Make sure text is easy to read against the background.

Publishing & Sharing Your Site

When your site is ready to share, you need to publish it. Your site is not visible to anyone until you publish.

How to Publish

Step 1: Click the Publish button in the top-right corner of the editor.

Google Sites editor ⚙ ⋮ Publish

Step 2: Under “Who can visit your published site,” select Not listed. Do not choose “Public” or “Restricted” — “Not listed” means anyone with the link can view it, but it won’t appear in search results.

Step 3: Click Publish.

Getting the Right Link

Step 4: After publishing, click the small dropdown arrow next to the Publish button and select View published site. Copy the URL from your browser’s address bar.

Google Sites editor ⚙ ⋮ Publish ▾

Make Sure You Have the Right Link

The published URL looks like:
sites.google.com/view/your-site-name

If the URL contains /edit or /d/, you are looking at the editing link, not the published site. I will not be able to view your site with the editing link. Go back and copy the URL from the published view instead.

Submit this link in Canvas when your project is due. Your site must stay live through the end of the semester.

General Tips

  • Save is automatic

    Google Sites saves your work as you go, just like Google Docs. You don’t need to manually save.

  • Preview before publishing

    Click the eye icon (👁) in the top toolbar to preview how your site will look to visitors. Check it on both desktop and mobile views.

  • You can republish anytime

    After your first publish, any changes you make in the editor won’t go live until you click Publish again. This means you can keep editing without visitors seeing unfinished work.

  • Keep it readable

    Choose a clean theme, use headings to organize your content, and don’t overcrowd pages. Your classmates will be exploring these on presentation day — make it easy for them.
